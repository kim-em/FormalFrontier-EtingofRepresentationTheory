## 2.8. Quivers

**Definition 2.8.1.** A **quiver** $Q$ is a directed graph, possibly with self-loops and/or multiple edges between two vertices.

**Example 2.8.2.**

$$\bullet \longrightarrow \bullet \longleftarrow \bullet$$
$$\uparrow$$
$$\bullet$$

We denote the set of vertices of the quiver $Q$ as $I$ and the set of edges as $E$. For an edge $h \in E$, let $h'$, $h''$ denote the source and target of $h$, respectively:

$$\bullet_{h'} \xrightarrow{h} \bullet_{h''}$$

**Definition 2.8.3.** A **representation of a quiver** $Q$ is an assignment to each vertex $i \in I$ of a vector space $V_i$ and to each edge $h \in E$ of a linear map $x_h : V_{h'} \longrightarrow V_{h''}$.
