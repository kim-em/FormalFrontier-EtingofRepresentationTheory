## 2.11. Tensor products

In this subsection we recall the notion of tensor product of vector spaces, which will be extensively used below.

**Definition 2.11.1.** The **tensor product** $V \otimes W$ of vector spaces $V$ and $W$ over a field $k$ is the quotient of the space $V * W$ whose basis is given by formal symbols $v \otimes w$, $v \in V$, $w \in W$, by the subspace spanned by the elements

$$
(v_1 + v_2) \otimes w - v_1 \otimes w - v_2 \otimes w,
$$
$$
v \otimes (w_1 + w_2) - v \otimes w_1 - v \otimes w_2,
$$
$$
av \otimes w - a(v \otimes w),
$$
$$
v \otimes aw - a(v \otimes w),
$$

where $v \in V, w \in W, a \in k$.

**Exercise 2.11.2.** Show that $V \otimes W$ can be equivalently defined as the quotient of the free abelian group $V \bullet W$ generated by $v \otimes w$, $v \in V, w \in W$ by the subgroup generated by

$$
(v_1 + v_2) \otimes w - v_1 \otimes w - v_2 \otimes w,
$$
$$
v \otimes (w_1 + w_2) - v \otimes w_1 - v \otimes w_2,
$$
$$
av \otimes w - v \otimes aw,
$$

where $v \in V, w \in W, a \in k$.

The elements $v \otimes w \in V \otimes W$, for $v \in V, w \in W$ are called pure tensors. Note that in general, there are elements of $V \otimes W$ which are not pure tensors.
