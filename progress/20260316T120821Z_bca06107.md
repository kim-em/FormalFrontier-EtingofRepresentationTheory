## Accomplished

Proved two key results from Chapter 3 Section 5 (issue #620):

1. **Theorem 3.5.4** (`Etingof.structure_mod_radical`): For a finite-dimensional algebra A over an algebraically closed field k with complete set of pairwise nonisomorphic irreducible representations V_i, A/Rad(A) is isomorphic to the product of End_k(V_i). The proof constructs the algebra map phi: A -> prod End(V_i) via the density theorem, then shows ker(phi) = Rad(A) by:
   - Rad(A) <= ker(phi): the Jacobson radical annihilates all semisimple modules (IsSemisimpleModule.jacobson_le_annihilator)
   - ker(phi) <= Rad(A): for each maximal left ideal J, A/J is simple hence isomorphic to some V_j by completeness; since a in ker(phi) annihilates V_j, it annihilates A/J, so a in J

   **Universe fix**: Changed A and V from independent `Type*` to shared `Type u` so that `h_complete` can be applied to quotients `A/J` (which live at the same universe as A).

2. **Corollary 3.5.5** (`Etingof.sum_dim_sq_le_dim`): The sum of squares of dimensions of irreducible representations is at most dim(A). Uses the density theorem's surjectivity and finrank bounds.
