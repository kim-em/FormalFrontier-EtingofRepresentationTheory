### Analysis of remaining sorry

The `compatible_product_decomp` sorry requires proving that V × W has a compatible chain basis — i.e., a Jordan-type basis for the swap operator where each chain starts from a pure element (v,0) or (0,w). This ensures each cyclic ℂ[X]-summand is a product submodule.

Key obstacles:
- **Generator replacement**: Each cyclic generator (v,w) can be replaced by a pure element of the same X-order, but showing the replaced generators still form a direct sum decomposition requires careful linear algebra
- **Projections don't preserve complements**: π_V(M₁) ⊕ π_V(M₂) ≠ V in general for M₁ ⊕ M₂ = V × W, so the approach of projecting X-invariant subspaces does not work
- **No direct Mathlib API**: There is no `Submodule.finrank_prod` or `Submodule.prodEquiv` for product submodules; had to construct LinearEquiv manually
