## Accomplished

Formalized Definition 9.7.1 (Morita equivalence) and Definition 9.7.2 (basic algebra) for Chapter 9.

- **Definition 9.7.1**: `Etingof.MoritaEquivalent A B` defined as `Nonempty (ModuleCat.{max u v} A ≌ ModuleCat.{max u v} B)` — two rings are Morita equivalent if their module categories are equivalent. Uses full module category (book restricts to finite-dimensional modules, but Mathlib lacks a standalone finite-dimensional module category).

- **Definition 9.7.2**: `Etingof.IsBasicAlgebra k A` defined as all simple A-modules being one-dimensional over k (`Module.finrank k M = 1`). Uses the "all simple modules are 1-dimensional" characterization rather than the Jacobson radical quotient characterization, as it's more directly usable downstream.

Both definitions build successfully with no `sorry` in definition bodies.
