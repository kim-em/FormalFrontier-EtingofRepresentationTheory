**Proof strategy**: Strong induction on `finrank ℂ W` via a helper lemma `sl2_trivial_of_casimir_zero_aux`:

- **Base case (dim 0)**: Module is subsingleton, trivially done.
- **Irreducible case**: Casimir acts as `n(n+2)·id` on irreducible `V_n` (via `casimir_on_irreducible_scalar`). If C=0, then `n(n+2)=0`, forcing `n=0`. The 1-dimensional module has trivial sl(2)-action since h, e, f all kill the unique (up to scalar) primitive vector.
- **Reducible case**: Find a proper nonzero Lie submodule N. Both N and W/N have strictly smaller dimension and inherit C=0. By induction, sl(2) acts trivially on both. Apply `sl2_acts_trivially_of_quotient_and_sub`.
