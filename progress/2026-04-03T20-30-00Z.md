## Current frontier

The remaining sorry is at line ~1160 in `Problem6_9_1.lean`, in the hard case of
`off_diagonal_nilpotent_product_decomp` where:
- `h1 : ker A ≤ range B` (every kernel element of A is in the range of B)
- `h2 : ker B ≤ range A` (every kernel element of B is in the range of A)
- `hker : 2 ≤ dim(ker A) + dim(ker B)`
- `_hAB : IsNilpotent (A.comp B)`

This is the case that requires the compatible chain basis argument from Problem 6.9.1(c):
1. Define X(v,w) = (Bw, Av) on V × W — nilpotent with dim(ker X) ≥ 2
2. PID decomposition gives ≥ 2 cyclic summands
3. Compatible generator lemma: each generator can be chosen in V×{0} or {0}×W
4. With compatible generators, splitting chains gives product-compatible decomposition

Key difficulty: projections of X-invariant complementary subspaces of V×W are NOT
automatically complementary in V and W. The compatible generator replacement is
essential — cannot be bypassed with simpler approaches.
