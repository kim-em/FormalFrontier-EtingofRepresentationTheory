### Issue #2124 (SYT entry comparison for polytabloid_syt_dominance)
- Analyzed PQ decomposition: σ_{T₂} = σ_{T₁}⁻¹ · q · σ_{T₁}² · p
- Showed column_perm_dominance handles q-part but p (row permutation) can genuinely change cumulative counts
- Investigated transfer approach (polytabloid → polytabloidTab): FAILS because the two definitions use different column permutation conventions. polytabloid uses T-relative κ_T = σ_T⁻¹ b_λ σ_T, while polytabloidTab uses canonical b_λ with q⁻¹. The natural map φ(a) = a·[id] gives φ(polytabloid(T)) = |P_λ| · κ_T · [σ_T] ≠ |P_λ| · b_λ · [σ_T] = |P_λ| · polytabloidTab(T)

- Group algebra version (polytabloid_linearIndependent) remains blocked on either the dominance lemma or a novel transfer argument

### Issue #2179 (compatible_product_decomp sorry)
- Extensive analysis of 4 proof approaches:
  1. **Comap approach**: Set V_i = A⁻¹(W_i) after decomposing W via AB. Fails because comap A W₁ ∩ comap A W₂ = ker A ≠ {0}. Works ONLY when ker A = 0.
  2. **Range approach**: Set V_i = B(W_i). Fails because B(W₁) ∩ B(W₂) ≠ {0} in general.
  3. **Linked pair**: Find v₀ ∈ ker(BA) with Av₀ ≠ 0, creating a 2-dim sub-rep. The complement needs AB-invariant qW, but nilpotent operators don't always have invariant complements.
  4. **Pure generator replacement** (book's approach, Problem 6.9.1(c)): Requires PID module theory to show cyclic submodules with pure generators are direct summands. Estimated ~200 lines of new infrastructure.
- Recommended decomposition into 4 sub-issues (comap special case, PID summand lemma, product subspace lemma, assembly)
