## Accomplished

### Proved `exists_character_in_rep` (#2036) - sorry count 2→1 in Theorem5_27_1.lean

Proved that for any simple FDRep W of A ⋊[φ] G (Mackey machine context), there exists a
character χ : A →* ℂˣ such that the χ-weight space of W is nonzero.

**Proof strategy** (following the book's approach):
1. Restrict W to A via ρ_A = W.ρ ∘ SemidirectProduct.inl
2. By Maschke's theorem (A finite, ℂ char 0), ρ_A.asModule is semisimple over ℂ[A]
3. Get a simple ℂ[A]-submodule m
4. Since A is commutative and ℂ is algebraically closed, dim_ℂ(m) = 1 (Schur + commutativity)
5. Pick nonzero v ∈ m; for each a ∈ A, ρ_A(a)(v) = χ(a)·v for a unique scalar χ(a)
6. χ is multiplicative (by uniqueness) and units-valued (ρ_A(a) invertible), giving χ : A →* ℂˣ
7. v ∈ weightSpace φ W χ, so the weight space is nonzero

**Key technical challenges overcome:**
- Type class management: `FiniteDimensional ℂ ↥m` (submodule of asModule), `Nontrivial ↥m`,
  `IsMulCommutative (MonoidAlgebra ℂ A)` all required explicit registration
- Connecting `W.ρ ⟨a, 1⟩` (the FDRep action) to `MonoidAlgebra.of ℂ A a • v` (the asModule
  MonoidAlgebra action) required careful `rw` through `Representation.asAlgebraHom_of`
- Scalar uniqueness uses `NoZeroSMulDivisors ℂ m` (from field acting on module)
- `smul_comm` for ℂ and MonoidAlgebra ℂ A actions (from IsScalarTower)

**Mathlib APIs used:**
- `IsSemisimpleModule.exists_simple_submodule` (Maschke)
- `IsSimpleModule.finrank_eq_one_of_isMulCommutative` (Schur + commutativity → 1-dim)
- `MonoidAlgebra.commSemiring` (ℂ[A] commutative for commutative A)
- `finrank_eq_one_iff_of_nonzero'` (1-dim ↔ every element is scalar multiple)
