## Next step

- Prove `hookWalkWeight_col_sum`: ∑_{u ∈ μ.cells} w(u.1, u.2, c) = HP(μ)/HP(μ\c)
- This is the column identity from the GNW (1979) hook walk paper
- Approach options:
  1. By induction on |μ|, relating w_μ to w_{μ\c₀} for another corner c₀
  2. By expressing HP/HP(μ\c) as ∏_{affected cells} h/(h-1) and showing the column sum matches this product
  3. The algebraic identity ∑_c ∏_{b<j_c} h(i_c,b)/(h(i_c,b)-1) × ∏_{a<i_c} h(a,j_c)/(h(a,j_c)-1) = |μ| could be proved directly without hook walks
- Key structural fact: outer corners are in distinct rows and distinct columns

## Blockers

- The column identity is a deep combinatorial result. Multiple approaches were attempted but none completed:
  - Direct Fubini with sum interchange works but requires identifying column sums with HP ratio
  - Induction on |μ| is complicated by new corners appearing and hook length changes upon corner removal
  - The correction factors when relating F_μ(c) to F_{μ'}(c) are cell-dependent (h+1)(h-1)/h²
