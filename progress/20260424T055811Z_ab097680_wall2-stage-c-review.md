## Findings

### Question 1 — Soundness: does `dTildeCast` commute `▸`-casts on the dimension equation with `LinearEquiv.funCongrLeft`?

**PASS.**

The refactor replaces each `ha ▸ hb ▸ F` cast inside `dTildeRepMap`
(line 2265–2298) with `dTildeCast ha hb F`, where

```
dTildeCast hp hq F =
  (LinearEquiv.funCongrLeft ℂ ℂ (finCongr hq)).toLinearMap ∘ₗ
    F ∘ₗ (LinearEquiv.funCongrLeft ℂ ℂ (finCongr hp.symm)).toLinearMap
```

(lines 2250–2255). `finCongr h` is val-preserving, so each
`funCongrLeft (finCongr _)` is pointwise a val-preserving reindexing.
`dTildeCast_apply` (line 2257–2262) proves the pointwise formula
`dTildeCast hp hq F x i = F (fun j => x ⟨j.val, _⟩) ⟨i.val, _⟩` by
`rfl`, confirming the reduction is definitional.

`dTildeTransportEquiv k m v` (line 2902–2912) is defined as
`LinearEquiv.funCongrLeft ℂ ℂ (finCongr hdim)`, where
`hdim : dTildeDim k m v.toFin = v.dim m`. Its pointwise action is also
a val-preserving reindexing.

Thus both sides of the transport equation reduce to val-preserving
compositions of `funCongrLeft`s applied to the primitive helpers
(`starEmbed1`, `starEmbed2`, `d5tildeGamma`, `dTildePathId`). Since the
primitives are the **same actual `LinearMap`s** on both sides of the
transport (not reindexed copies), the composition equals `rfl` after the
correct `dTildeRepMap` branch is selected.

I traced this for two of the four hard arrow cases:

* **`pathMid i → pathMid j` (case at line 3090)**: the helper lemma
  `dTildeRepMap_path_id` selects the 4th `if`-branch
  (`3 ≤ a.val ∧ a.val + 1 = b.val ∧ b.val ≤ k + 3`) via three
  `dif_neg` rewrites. Remaining goal is
  `dTildeCast hp hq (dTildePathId m) (transport x) = transport (dTildePathId m x)`,
  which reduces to `x ⟨i.val, _⟩` on both sides via `funCongrLeft`
  composition. `rfl` closes it — and Lean accepts this in the merged
  code.
* **`rightLeaf2 → branchRight` (case at line 3121)**: selects
  `dTildeRepMap_starEmbed2_right` via five `dif_neg`s + `dif_pos`.
  Goal reduces to
  `dTildeCast hp hq (starEmbed2 m) (transport x) = transport (starEmbed2 m x)`,
  val-preserving on both sides, closed by `rfl`.

The other six cases (incl. the remaining two "hard" ones,
`pathMid i → branchRight` at line 3104 and `rightLeaf1 → branchRight`
at line 3117) follow the same pattern and were accepted by the kernel
(verified by successful `lake build`). The proof structure is uniform:
`show dTildeRepMap k m _ _ _ = _` to expose the match, `rw` with the
branch-unfolding helper, then `rfl`.

### Question 2 — Consistency with Stage C main math: no circularity?

**PASS.**

`dTildeRep_isIndecomposable` (lines 3129–3185) depends on
`dTildeRep'_isIndecomposable` via transport, not recursion:

1. `hind := dTildeRep'_isIndecomposable k m` (line 3134) is obtained
   directly from the `DTildeVertex`-indexed proof (line 2446–2887,
   proved independently without reference to `dTildeRep_isIndecomposable`).
2. Nontriviality transports via `dTildeRep'_obj_eq_toFin` and `▸` on
   a proof-level equality between object types (line 3139).
3. Invariance transports via `rw [← dTildeRep_mapLinear_transport k m e x]`
   (lines 3150, 3156), which turns
   `transport ((dTildeRep' k m).mapLinear e x) ∈ W₁ b.toFin` into
   `(dTildeRep k m).mapLinear _ (transport x) ∈ W₁ b.toFin`, then
   `hW₁` closes.
4. Complementarity transports via `Submodule.orderIsoMapComap` on
   `dTildeTransportEquiv` (line 3158–3161).
5. The "bot implies bot" direction uses the same order-iso machinery
   (lines 3163–3170).

No theorem in the chain
`dTildeRep_mapLinear_transport → dTildeRep_isIndecomposable`
references `dTildeRep_isIndecomposable` itself. The only
transport-only assumption is the well-formed fact
`dTildeDim k m v.toFin = v.dim m` (proved by `dTildeDim_eq_ofFin_dim`
+ `DTildeVertex.ofFin_toFin`, both non-sorry).

**PASS.**

The top-level statements a mathematician would read are:

* `dTildeRep_isIndecomposable (k m : ℕ) : IsIndecomposable (dTildeRep k m)`

   `show dTildeRepMap k m _ _ _ = _` pattern in each of the 8

   Keep as-is.
