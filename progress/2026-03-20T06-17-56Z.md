## Accomplished

Proved `dynkin_edge_count` (#1355): a Dynkin diagram on n vertices has exactly n-1 edges (it's a tree). The proof combines two bounds:

1. **Upper bound** (edgeCount ≤ n-1): The all-ones vector gives B(1,...,1) = 2n - 2·edgeCount > 0 by positive definiteness, so edgeCount < n. Since the degree sum is even (by symmetry), edgeCount ≤ n-1.

2. **Lower bound** (n-1 ≤ edgeCount): Convert the adjacency matrix to a Mathlib `SimpleGraph`, establish connectivity by building `SimpleGraph.Reachable` from the list-based paths in `IsDynkinDiagram`, then apply `Connected.card_vert_le_card_edgeSet_add_one`. The handshaking lemma (`sum_degrees_eq_twice_card_edges`) relates `edgeCount` to `edgeFinset.card`.
