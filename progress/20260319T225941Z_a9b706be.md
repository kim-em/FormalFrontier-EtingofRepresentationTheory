### Proved `hσ_inj` (block assignment injectivity) — sorry-free

The proof that `σ : ι → Fin n` (mapping each simple module to its Wedderburn-Artin block) is injective. The proof constructs `Module (Matrix (Fin (d l)) (Fin (d l)) k)` instances on both `M i` and `M j`, proves they are `IsSimpleModule` by showing A-submodule and Mat-submodule lattices coincide, applies `IsSimpleRing.nonempty_linearEquiv_of_isSimpleModule` for the Mat-linear equiv, then converts to an A-linear equiv.

Key techniques:
- `matAct` construction: `mat • m := lft(WA.symm(Pi.single l mat)) • m`
- Decomposition lemma: `a • m = matAct ((WA (π a)) l) m` (A-action = Mat-action on active block)
- Custom `hpi_single_mul` helper to work around dependent type inference issues with `Pi.single_mul`
- `simp [Pi.mul_apply, ...]` pattern instead of `rw [Pi.single_eq_of_ne ...]` for dependent Pi types

### `hrank` i=j case — not completed (substantially harder)

The rank-1 property (E₁₁ in block σ(i) acts with rank 1 on M_i) requires:
1. Constructing Module + IsSimpleModule over Mat (same as hσ_inj — ~70 lines of duplication)
2. Proving `IsScalarTower k (Matrix ...) (M i)` (~15 lines)
3. Getting k-linear equiv `fk : M i ≃ₗ[k] (Fin d → k)` and proving k-linearity
4. Transferring smulRange via fk to image(E₁₁) on k^d
5. Computing finrank of image(E₁₁) on k^d = 1

Total: ~120-150 lines of new code, with significant dependent-type challenges at each step. Three approaches were investigated but all require the same Module construction + dimension transfer.
