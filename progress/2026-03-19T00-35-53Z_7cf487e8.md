## Current frontier

The remaining sorry (`exists_young_symmetrizer_nontrivial`) states: for any simple
C[S_n]-module M, some Young symmetrizer c_λ acts nontrivially on M.

This requires proving that the two-sided ideal generated by {c_λ : λ ⊢ n} equals
C[S_n], which follows from #partitions(n) = #Wedderburn_blocks. The standard proof
uses: #partitions = #conjugacy_classes = dim(center of C[S_n]) = #blocks.

Missing Mathlib infrastructure:
- Surjectivity of `Equiv.Perm.partition` (every partition realized as a cycle type) —
  can be proved using `Equiv.Perm.exists_with_cycleType_iff` from Mathlib
- `Fintype.card (ConjClasses (Perm (Fin n))) = Fintype.card (Nat.Partition n)` —
  follows from `partition_eq_of_isConj` + surjectivity
- dim(center of k[G]) = #conjugacy_classes — needs center of group algebra = span of class sums
- #Wedderburn_blocks = dim(center) — needs center of matrix ring = scalar matrices
