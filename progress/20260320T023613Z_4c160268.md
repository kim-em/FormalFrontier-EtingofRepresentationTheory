## Accomplished

Worked on issue #1275: Stage 3.2 Ch5 polytabloid basis (linear independence + straightening).

1. **Discovered `exists_maximal_for_eval` was incorrectly stated**: The evaluation matrix
   M[T,T'] = c_λ(σ_T⁻¹ · σ_{T'}) can be nonzero in both directions for distinct T, T'.
   Concrete counterexample: λ=(2,1,1), n=4, T₂=[[0,2],[1],[3]], T₃=[[0,3],[1],[2]],
   σ_{T₂}⁻¹·σ_{T₃} = (23) ∈ Q_λ. Removed the incorrect lemma.

2. **Proved `youngSymmetrizer_rowPerm_coeff`**: For any p ∈ P_λ (row subgroup),
   c_λ(p) = 1. This is a key building block for future proofs — it shows the Young
   symmetrizer evaluates to 1 on all row permutations.

3. **Documented correct proof strategies**: Added extensive comments explaining:
   - Why the evaluation-at-σ_T approach fails (with counterexample)
   - The correct tabloid basis triangularity approach (James Ch. 3)
   - The Garnir relations needed for the straightening lemma
