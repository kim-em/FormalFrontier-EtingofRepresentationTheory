## Accomplished

Proved Theorem 2.1.1(i) — classification of irreducible sl(2,C) representations (issue #1193). This removes the uniqueness sorry entirely, leaving only the complete reducibility sorry in part (ii).

**What was proved:**
- `exists_highest_eigenvalue`: any fd sl2-module has a maximal h-eigenvalue
- `exists_primitiveVector`: any irreducible sl2-module has a primitive (highest weight) vector
- `sl2_decomp`: every sl2 element decomposes as C-linear combo of h, e, f
- `lie_primitiveOrbit_mem`: the f-orbit of a primitive vector is Lie-invariant
- `primitiveOrbit_lieInvariant`, `primitiveOrbit_span_eq_top`: the f-orbit span is all of V (by irreducibility)
- `primitiveOrbit_linearIndependent`: f-orbit vectors have distinct h-eigenvalues
- `primitiveOrbit_basis`: the f-orbit is a basis (dimension = weight + 1)
- `sl2_irrep_equiv`: two irreducible modules with same primitive weight are isomorphic as Lie modules (basis-to-basis map preserves all Lie brackets)
- `Theorem_2_1_1_i`: for each d >= 1, there is exactly one irreducible sl2-representation of dimension d (existence from Sl2Irrep + uniqueness from highest weight theory)

**Key techniques:**
- `Module.End.exists_eigenvalue` (algebraically closed field) for highest eigenvalue existence
- `eigenvectors_linearIndependent'` to show infinitely many eigenvalues is impossible in finite dimension
- `IsSl2Triple.HasPrimitiveVectorWith` formulas to verify Lie module isomorphism (map_lie')
- `Basis.mk` + `Basis.equiv` for the linear equivalence
- `IsSimpleOrder.eq_bot_or_eq_top` for irreducibility
