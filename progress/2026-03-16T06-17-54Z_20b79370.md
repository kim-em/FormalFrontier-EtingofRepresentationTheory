## Accomplished

Proved both parts of Lemma 3.8.2 (endomorphisms of indecomposable representations):

1. **Part (i)**: Any endomorphism of a finite-dimensional indecomposable representation is either bijective or nilpotent. Proof uses the Fitting decomposition (`isCompl_iSup_ker_pow_iInf_range_pow`) to get complementary A-submodules ker(θ^n) and range(θ^n). Indecomposability forces one to be trivial.

2. **Part (ii)**: A finite sum of nilpotent endomorphisms of an indecomposable representation is nilpotent. Proof by induction on the number of summands, using part (i) for the dichotomy. The contradiction in the bijective case uses: the inverse composed with each summand must be nilpotent (by part (i)), but they sum to the identity, and (1 - nilpotent) is a unit, contradicting nilpotency.
