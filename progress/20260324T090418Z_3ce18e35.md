## Current frontier

One sorry remains: `q2_nontrivial_decomp` at line 1062 in `Problem6_9_1.lean`.

### Analysis of approaches (all tried, all blocked)

1. **B-image approach** (V₁ = B(W₁), V₂ = B(W₂)): Fails because ker B can mix W₁/W₂ components, making V₁ ∩ V₂ ≠ ⊥, and B not surjective so V₁ + V₂ ≠ V.

2. **A-preimage approach** (V₁ = A⁻¹(W₁), V₂ = A⁻¹(W₂)): V₁ ∩ V₂ = ker A (nonzero), and V₁ + V₂ = V iff range A is compatible with W₁ ⊕ W₂ (not guaranteed).

3. **Fitting ker/range decomposition** (ker(AB) ⊕ range(AB) for W, ker(BA) ⊕ range(BA) for V): The intertwining relations make these compatible, BUT Disjoint ker(BA) range(BA) does not follow from Disjoint ker(AB) range(AB). And when AB = 0, the W-decomposition is trivial.

4. **Product space with nilpotent_nontrivial_decomp**: Applying nilpotent_nontrivial_decomp to X on V × W gives M₁ ⊕ M₂, but projections of complementary subspaces are NOT necessarily complementary.

5. **Product space intersections** (pV = M₁ ∩ (V×{0})): Gives disjoint V-subspaces but NOT codisjoint — mixed elements prevent spanning.

6. **Direct case analysis** (AB = 0, BA = 0, etc.): AB = BA = 0 works via decomp_of_AB_BA_zero, but AB = 0, BA ≠ 0 has the same fundamental obstruction.

### The correct approach (not yet implemented)

**Compatible chain basis theorem**: The off-diagonal operator X(v,w) = (Bw,Av) has the property that its chains alternate between V and W (since X maps V→W and W→V). A chain basis with homogeneous generators (each in V×{0} or {0}×W) can be constructed by choosing the "free" component to be 0 at each lifting step. With ≥ 2 chains (from dim ker X ≥ 2), grouping gives the Q₂-decomposition.

This requires ~200-300 lines of Lean: defining X, proving nilpotency, constructing homogeneous chains from ker X elements, showing independence, and extracting the V/W decomposition.
