## Next step

The `alternatingKostka_norm_sq_eq_one` sorry requires substantial symmetric function theory infrastructure. The most tractable approach:

1. **Bialternant formula** (a_{λ+ρ} = Δ·s_λ): This is likely the most accessible piece, connecting alternants to Schur polynomials via the Vandermonde determinant.
2. **Cauchy identity**: ∏_{i,j}(1-x_iy_j)^{-1} = ∑_λ s_λ(x)s_λ(y). This is a core identity in symmetric function theory.
3. **Exponential formula / coefficient extraction**: Connect P_σ products to complete homogeneous symmetric polynomials.

Alternatively, consider whether the proof can be restructured to avoid this identity entirely (e.g., by proving the character expansion first through a different route).
