### Mathematical analysis of the sorry

The identity `∑_ν L²_{νλ} = 1` requires two components:

1. **Character orthonormality + non-isomorphism**: Already available:
   - `spechtModuleFDRep_simple`: Specht modules are simple FDReps
   - `spechtModule_noniso`: Distinct partitions give non-isomorphic Specht modules
   - `FDRep.char_orthonormal` (Mathlib): ⟨χ_V, χ_W⟩ = δ_{V≅W}
   - Together these give: ⟨θ_λ, θ_λ⟩ = ∑_ν L²_{νλ}

2. **Vandermonde coefficient orthogonality**: ⟨θ_λ, θ_λ⟩ = 1. NOT yet proved.
   - Equivalent to: (1/n!) ∑_σ ([x^{λ+ρ}](Δ·P_σ))² = 1
   - Requires the power sum Cauchy identity (issue #1622)
   - Or equivalently: Cauchy-Binet for formal power series + alternant coefficient extraction

### Why simpler approaches don't work

- **Direct expansion**: ⟨θ_λ, θ_λ⟩ via permutation module inner products gives ∑_ν L²_{νλ} tautologically — no new information
- **Dimension counting** (∑ (dim V_λ)² = n!): Gives RSK identity but doesn't connect to θ_λ
- **Triangularity alone**: L is lower triangular with ±1 diagonal, but this only shows ∑ L² ≥ 1, not ≤ 1
- **Character theory without Cauchy**: Can't compute ⟨θ_λ, θ_λ⟩ = 1 without connecting polynomial and group-theoretic inner products
