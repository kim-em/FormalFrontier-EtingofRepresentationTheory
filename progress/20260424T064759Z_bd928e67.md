## The corrected identity

### Definitions (as in `SpechtModuleBasis.lean`)

- `σ : S_n` column-standard, `rowInvCount'(σ) > 0`.
- `w ∈ S_n` with `w ≠ 1`, `w ∉ Q_λ` (column subgroup), `w ∉ P_λ` (row subgroup).
- `ψ_τ := generalizedPolytabloidTab τ = Σ_{q ∈ Q_λ} sign(q) · δ_{[q⁻¹ τ]}`.
- `TP(w, σ) := twistedPolytabloid w σ = Σ_{q ∈ Q_λ} sign(q) · δ_{[w q⁻¹ σ]}`.
- `L := span_ℂ {ψ_τ : τ col-std, (tabloidStrictDominates la σ τ) ∨ (toTabloid τ =
  toTabloid σ ∧ rowInvCount'(τ) < rowInvCount'(σ))}`.

Observation: `TP(w, σ) = w · ψ_σ` under the left S_n-action on M^λ (left
translation on tabloids: `w · δ_{[α]} = δ_{[w α]}`). So TP(w, σ) ∈ V^λ
(the Specht module) since V^λ is an S_n-submodule.

### Core identity (proposed C.1 replacement)

**Theorem (Tabloid-support straightening).** Under the above hypotheses,
TP(w, σ) admits a decomposition

    TP(w, σ) = Σ_{[α] ∈ supp_≼(TP(w, σ))} c_{[α]} · ψ_{syt([α])}

where:

- `supp_≼(TP(w, σ))` is the **effective tabloid support** of TP(w, σ) — i.e.
  the set of tabloids `[α]` appearing with nonzero net coefficient in the
  tabloid-basis expansion `TP(w, σ) = Σ_q sign(q) δ_{[w q⁻¹ σ]}`;
- `syt([α])` is the unique **standard Young tableau** with tabloid `[α]` (row-
  and column-increasing representative of [α]);
- `c_{[α]} ∈ ℤ` is the integer coefficient computed by the iterative leading-
  tabloid elimination below (Algorithm A);
- **every** `[α] ∈ supp_≼(TP(w, σ))` satisfies `[α] ≼ [σ]` (tabloid weakly
  dominated by [σ]) — this is the **Support Bound** sub-lemma below.

Each τ = `syt([α])` appearing in the sum lies in the spanning set of `L`:

- If `[α] ≺ [σ]` strictly: `tabloidStrictDominates la σ τ` (Or.inl).
- If `[α] = [σ]`: `toTabloid τ = toTabloid σ` and `rowInvCount'(τ) = 0 <
  rowInvCount'(σ)` (since τ is standard, so has no row-inversions, and σ has
  at least one by hypothesis `hrp`). (Or.inr.)

Hence **TP(w, σ) ∈ L**, which is the conclusion of
`garnir_twisted_in_lower_span`.

### Algorithm A (leading-tabloid elimination)

Input: an element `v ∈ V^λ` given as a finite tabloid-basis support
`{([α_i], b_i) : i = 1..k}` where `v = Σ_i b_i δ_{[α_i]}`.

Output: a decomposition `v = Σ_j c_j ψ_{syt([β_j])}` with `[β_j]`s distinct
and appearing in the support.

Procedure:
1. If all `b_i = 0`, return the empty decomposition.
2. Let `[α_max]` be the tabloid in `supp(v)` maximal in dominance order, with
   coefficient `b_max`. (Well-defined: the support is a finite subset of the
   tabloid dominance poset.)
3. Set τ := syt([α_max]) — the standard Young tableau representative.
4. Emit the term `b_max · ψ_τ`.
5. Recurse on `v' := v − b_max · ψ_τ`. Correctness: `ψ_τ` in the tabloid
   basis has the form `δ_{[α_max]} + (combination of strictly-dominated
   tabloids)` (James Lemma 8.5: `ψ_σ` for σ col-std has leading tabloid
   [σ]; the ±1 coefficient on `δ_{[σ]}` comes from `q = 1 ∈ Q_λ`). So `v'`
   has zero coefficient on `δ_{[α_max]}` and every remaining tabloid is
   strictly dominated by `[α_max]`.

Termination: tabloid dominance is a finite partial order (each tabloid has
only finitely many dominators), so the recursion terminates.

### The Support Bound sub-lemma

**Lemma.** Let σ be column-standard, `w ∈ S_n` any permutation. Every
tabloid `[α]` with nonzero net coefficient in the tabloid-basis expansion of
`TP(w, σ) = Σ_q sign(q) δ_{[w q⁻¹ σ]}` satisfies `tabloidDominates la σ α`
(i.e. `[α] ≼ [σ]`).

Status: **conjectural but well-supported**; classical background plus the
counter-example validations below give strong evidence, but a formal proof
still needs to be written.

Intuition: TP(w, σ) ∈ V^λ. James' theorem says V^λ has basis
`{ψ_τ : τ standard}`, and any element of V^λ has tabloid-basis support
bounded in dominance by the tabloid of its "leading" standard τ. For col-std
σ with rowInv(σ) > 0, the σ itself is *not* standard (since rowInv(σ) > 0
means σ has a row descent). Applying a "neither" w may in principle raise
the leading tabloid above [σ] for individual summands `[w q⁻¹ σ]`, but in
the alternating sum over Q_λ those "high-tabloid" terms must cancel — see
the λ=(3,2) illustration below.

Two independent avenues to a formal proof:

1. **Direct tabloid-cumulative-count argument**: for each k and i,
   `tabloidCumulCount la α k i ≤ tabloidCumulCount la σ k i` for every α in
   the effective support. Analyze Σ_q sign(q) · 1_{cumulCount(wq⁻¹σ, k, i) > target}
   and show cancellation when target > cumulCount(σ, k, i).

2. **Via James straightening on ψ-basis**: write TP(w, σ) = Σ_τ c_τ ψ_τ (τ
   standard). Show that every τ with c_τ ≠ 0 has [τ] ≼ [σ]. Two-step: first
   show [τ] ≼ [wσ] (James' dominance bound on the S_n-action), then show
   [wσ] ≼ [σ] for col-std σ (this requires w ∉ P_λ ∪ Q_λ or appropriate
   cancellation).

Avenue 1 is more elementary and more directly Lean-formalizable; avenue 2
relies on classical Specht-module theorems that the project has not yet
proved. **Recommendation for the follow-up C.1 implementation: pursue
avenue 1 as a pure combinatorial lemma on the alternating sum.**

## The Support Bound: λ=(3,2) cancellation illustration

A test designed to probe the Support Bound beyond pigeonhole cases.
σ = [1,0,4,2,3] (col-std, rowInvCount' = 2, [σ] = [{0,1,3}|{2,4}]).
w' = σ⁻¹ = [1,0,3,4,2]. w' ∉ P_λ (w'(2)=3 crosses rows), w' ∉ Q_λ
(w'(0)=1 crosses cols).

Then [w'σ] = [id] = [{0,1,2}|{3,4}] **strictly dominates** [σ] (at k=2:
cumulCount = 3 vs 2). This would seemingly violate the Support Bound if
checked per-q.

Full TP computation:

    q=id:             w'σ = id. Tabloid [id]. sign +1.
    q=(0,3):          w'·(0,3)·σ. Tabloid [{0,2,4}|{1,3}]. sign −1.
    q=(1,4):          w'·(1,4)·σ = [2,1,0,3,4]. Tabloid [id]. sign −1.
    q=(0,3)(1,4):     Tabloid [{0,2,4}|{1,3}]. sign +1.

    TP(σ⁻¹, σ) = (+1 − 1) δ_[id] + (−1 + 1) δ_[{0,2,4}|{1,3}] = 0.

The [id] terms — which individually would have violated the Support Bound —
cancel pairwise in the alternating sum. The effective support is empty, so
Support Bound holds vacuously.

This illustrates the **cross-q cancellation mechanism** at the heart of the
Support Bound: individual summands `[w q⁻¹ σ]` may dominate [σ], but the
signed sum over Q_λ always cancels such terms when [α] ≻ [σ].

**Why cancellation happens**: for σ col-std, the set of q ∈ Q_λ producing a
given tabloid `[α]` with `[α] ≻ [σ]` forms a sign-zero union of Q_λ-cosets.
Intuitively: if `[w q⁻¹ σ] = [α]` for q and some q' ≠ q, then q·q'⁻¹ stabilizes
the tabloid [w · Q_λ · σ]-class, and sign(q·q'⁻¹) = ±1 depending on whether
the tabloid-stabilizer has sign-cancelling structure. For `[α] ≻ [σ]`, the
stabilizer inside Q_λ *does* have sign-cancelling structure — proving this
in full is the core of the Support Bound.

### C.1.a: Support Bound lemma (the hard part)

Statement:
```
private theorem twistedPolytabloid_support_bound
    (σ : Equiv.Perm (Fin n)) (hcs : isColumnStandard' n la σ)
    (w : Equiv.Perm (Fin n)) :
    ∀ α : Tabloid n la,
      (Finsupp.single_coeff α (twistedPolytabloid (la := la) w σ) ≠ 0) →
      tabloidDominates la σ (some_rep_of α)
```
(Exact Lean formalization — tabloid as an element of the basis, with
membership via the `TabloidRepresentation n la` Finsupp structure.)

Proof sketch (avenue 1): for each threshold (k, i), analyze the signed count
`Σ_{q ∈ Q_λ : cumulCount(wq⁻¹σ, k, i) > cumulCount(σ, k, i)} sign(q)` and
show it is zero. The argument uses Q_λ's structure as a product of symmetric
groups on columns, plus a pigeonhole on column entries.

Estimated difficulty: 8/10. ~150–250 Lean lines.

### C.1.b: Leading-tabloid elimination

State and prove Algorithm A correctness:
```
theorem tabloidSupport_straightening
    (v : TabloidRepresentation n la) (hv : v ∈ V^λ)
    (hv_supp : ∀ α ∈ support(v), tabloidDominates la σ α) :
    v ∈ L
```
Proof: strong induction on `srRank × rowInvCount'` lex order, subtracting
leading ψ_{syt(α_max)} at each step.

Estimated difficulty: 6/10. ~100–150 Lean lines. Key sub-lemma:
`ψ_{syt([α])}` has leading-in-dominance tabloid `[α]` (i.e., the tabloid
basis coefficient on `δ_{[α]}` is +1, and all other tabloids in ψ's support
are strictly dominated by `[α]`).

### C.1.c: Glue — instantiate for TP(w, σ)

```
private theorem garnir_twisted_in_lower_span (...) :
    twistedPolytabloid (la := la) w σ ∈ L := by
  apply tabloidSupport_straightening
  · exact TP_is_in_specht_module  -- since TP = w · ψ_σ and V^λ is S_n-invariant
  · intro α hα
    exact twistedPolytabloid_support_bound σ hcs w α hα
```

Estimated difficulty: 2/10. ~20 Lean lines.

## Interface notes for C.2 (issue #2520)

C.2's stated job: "prove each such τ satisfies the broadened disjunction".

With Algorithm A, the τ's are **standard Young tableaux** indexed by
tabloids `[α]` in the effective support of TP(w, σ). Specifically:

1. **Indexing**: τ = `sytPerm (toTabloid-class of α)` — the unique standard
   representative of the tabloid `[α]`.
2. **Dominance guarantee**: `tabloidDominates la σ τ`, i.e. `[τ] ≼ [σ]`,
   with equality iff `[α] = [σ]`.

C.2's proof — the classification — reduces to two sub-cases:

- If `toTabloid τ ≠ toTabloid σ`: since `[τ] ≼ [σ]` weakly and `[τ] ≠ [σ]`,
  we have `[τ] ≺ [σ]` strictly, giving `tabloidStrictDominates la σ τ` (Or.inl).
- If `toTabloid τ = toTabloid σ`: τ is a standard tableau, so `rowInvCount'(τ)
  = 0` (standard tableaux have row-increasing rows, hence no inversions).
  Combined with hypothesis `hrp : 0 < rowInvCount'(σ)`, this gives
  `rowInvCount'(τ) = 0 < rowInvCount'(σ)` (Or.inr).

Both branches are straightforward once the τ = SYT indexing is used. No
`q`-level or `ψ-equivalence`-level reasoning is needed in C.2 — that all
stays inside C.1's Algorithm A.

**Estimated C.2 difficulty**: 3/10. ~30–50 Lean lines. The only non-trivial
ingredient is `rowInvCount'(syt T) = 0` for any SYT T, which should be a
short lemma (an SYT has rows in increasing order by definition).

## Alternative directions (considered, not pursued)

1. **"Group fibres across ψ-equivalence"**: detect when `ψ_{τ₁} = ψ_{τ₂}`
   (as in `ψ_[1,0,3,2] = ψ_id` at Case I) or `ψ_τ = 0` (pigeonhole at
   Case II/III), and derive a coefficient formula on ψ-equivalence classes.
   **Rejected**: computing ψ-equivalences is combinatorially hard and
   fragile. The dominance-induction approach sidesteps the issue by
   computing the decomposition on the tabloid basis first, where two
   different τ's with `ψ_τ = ψ_{τ'}` reduce to the same leading tabloid
   automatically.

2. **Young-symmetrizer / left-translation identity**: express `TP(w, σ) =
   w · ψ_σ` and use Q_λ-averaging identities. This gives the key identity
   TP = w·ψ_σ but doesn't immediately yield a ψ_τ-basis decomposition.
   Useful as a starting point (noted in the core identity section) but not
   a standalone strategy.

3. **Weaker C.1 bypass — Neither-sum ∈ L directly**: show
   `Σ_{w Neither} sign(w) TP(w, σ) ∈ L` without per-w membership.
   **Rejected**: circularity. The Garnir identity gives
   `ψ_σ = −(Q-part) − (P\Q-part) − (Neither-sum)`, and the Q and P\Q
   parts simplify to `(1 + k + c) ψ_σ`, so `Neither-sum = −(1 + k + c) ψ_σ`.
   Showing `Neither-sum ∈ L` is thus equivalent (when `1 + k + c ≠ 0`) to
   showing `ψ_σ ∈ L`, which is the full theorem we're trying to prove.
   Direct per-w analysis is genuinely needed.

## Verification of the refutation of the issue-as-written

The original #2519 approach decomposed TP per-fibre of `q ↦ γ_{w,q}·(wq⁻¹σ)`,
claiming each fibre's partial sum is a ℂ-multiple of a single ψ_τ. The prior
refutation (`20260424T062351Z_811935af.md`) showed that at Case I above, all
four fibres are singletons with distinct τ(q)'s, so the "fibre partial sum" is
always a single tabloid — which cannot equal any nonzero ℂ-multiple of a ψ_τ
(supports differ: 1 tabloid vs 4). The current note replaces that approach
entirely; no attempt to salvage per-fibre reasoning.
