## Accomplished

Proved `complementW_mem_of_mul` (deliverable 1 of issue #1538): the augmentation
reindexing lemma showing that right translation preserves zero augmentation in
the complement W_μ submodule. The proof reindexes the sum via `Fintype.sum_equiv
(Equiv.mulRight h)`, then uses det multiplicativity and factors out the constant
`μ(det h⁻¹)⁻¹` to reduce to the hypothesis `∑ g, f(g) · μ(det g)⁻¹ = 0`.

Sorry count reduced from 5 to 4 in Theorem5_25_2.lean.

## Current frontier

Deliverables 2 and 3 of #1538 (`Theorem5_25_2_part1` and `Theorem5_25_2_part2`)
remain sorry'd. These require substantial character theory infrastructure:

1. **Frobenius character formula** for induced representations
2. **Conjugacy class decomposition** of GL₂(𝔽_q) into 4 types (scalar, unipotent, diagonal, elliptic)
3. **Character inner product computation** showing ⟨χ_V, χ_V⟩ = 1 (for χ₁ ≠ χ₂) or = 2 (for χ₁ = χ₂ = μ)
4. **Bridge from character inner product to Simple**: connecting ⟨χ,χ⟩ = 1 to `Simple` in `FDRep`
5. **Dimension computation**: dim V(χ₁,χ₂) = [G:B] = q+1 via group order formulas

None of this infrastructure currently exists in the codebase. Each item is a
multi-session effort. The `Simple` condition in `FDRep` is equivalent to
`IsSimpleOrder (Subobject X)` (Mathlib's `simple_iff_subobject_isSimpleOrder`),
which means showing the only sub-representations are ⊥ and ⊤.
