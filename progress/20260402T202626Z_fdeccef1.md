### Mathematical analysis

Extensively analyzed the proof approaches:

1. **Naive projection approach** (old code): defining pV = M₁.map fst, qV = M₂.map fst from X-invariant M₁, M₂ in V×W fails because map fst doesn't preserve IsCompl.

2. **Comap approach**: defining pV = N₁.comap A from AB-invariant N₁, N₂ in W works when A is injective (disjointness from ker A = ⊥) but fails for codisjointness when A is not surjective.

3. **Compatible chain basis** (book's approach, correct): X(v,w) = (Bw, Av) swaps V and W components. The key lemma: if X^k(v,w) = 0, then X^k(v,0) = 0 AND X^k(0,w) = 0 individually, because X^k(v,0) and X^k(0,w) have nonzero entries in disjoint positions (V vs W). This means PID cyclic summands can always use compatible generators (in V×{0} or {0}×W). With compatible generators, chain elements alternate between V and W, so splitting chains gives a product-compatible decomposition.

4. **Under Indecomposable hypothesis**: ker A ⊆ range B and ker B ⊆ range A imply ker X ⊆ range X, forcing the PID case (no elementary kernel/range decomposition available).
