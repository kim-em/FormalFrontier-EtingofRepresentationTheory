**What was proved:** When the Casimir operator C does NOT act as a constant c_irr on all of N, the eigenspace N' = N ∩ ker(C - c_irr) is a proper Lie submodule of N. By passing to the quotient V/N' (which has strictly smaller dimension), applying the IH to get complete reducibility of V/N', and pulling back a complement, we construct a Lie submodule T with N ⊓ T = N' and N ⊔ T = ⊤. Then the IH on T (smaller dimension) gives a complement of N'∩T in T, which maps to a complement of N in V.
