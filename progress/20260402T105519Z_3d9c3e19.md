### New proven lemma: `iso_of_surjection_with_trivial_kernel_head`

Given a surjective B₂-linear map f : P → B₂ where P is finitely generated and
the kernel satisfies K ≤ J(B₂)·K, proves P ≅ B₂ using:
- `Submodule.FG.eq_bot_of_le_jacobson_smul` (non-commutative Nakayama)
- `LinearEquiv.ofBijective` → `toModuleIso` for the isomorphism

### Proof decomposition

`basic_morita_regular_module_iso` now reduces to two clearly isolated sub-goals:

1. **`exists_surjection_with_trivial_kernel_head`** (sorry): Construct a B₂-linear
   surjection F(B₁) → B₂ whose kernel K satisfies K ≤ J(B₂)·K. Proof strategy:
   - Both F(B₁)/J·F(B₁) and B₂/J·B₂ are ≅ ⊕ᵢ Sᵢ (each simple once, by basicness + simple_of_equivalence)
   - Lift head iso to F(B₁) → B₂ using projectivity (Mathlib: `Functor.preservesProjectiveObjects_of_isEquivalence`)
   - Nakayama gives surjectivity; B₂ projective gives splitting; K/JK = 0 from head matching

2. **`Module.Finite B₂ (F.functor.obj ...)`** (sorry): F(B₁) is finitely generated
   as a B₂-module. Standard categorical fact (equivalences preserve finite generation).

### Infrastructure used

## Current frontier

## Next step

Work on `exists_surjection_with_trivial_kernel_head`. The proof should:
1. Define "head" of a module (quotient by J·M) and show it's semisimple
2. Show F preserves heads (F preserves maximal submodules → preserves radical)
3. Show heads of F(B₁) and B₂ are both ≅ ⊕ all simples (basicness + equivalence)
4. Lift the head isomorphism using Module.projective_lifting_property
5. Show the lift is surjective (Nakayama on cokernel)
6. Split (B₂ is projective) and show kernel has trivial head

Alternatively, a dimension-based approach could work if dim_k F(B₁) = dim_k B₂
can be established.
