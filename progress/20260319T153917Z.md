## Accomplished

Decomposed the monolithic sorry in `exists_orthogonal_idempotents_for_simples` (Theorem9_2_1.lean) into 5 well-documented sub-goals and proved 3 of them:

1. **hblock_exists** (PROVED): For each simple module M_j, there exists a unique Wedderburn-Artin block l such that the central idempotent c_l acts as identity on M_j. Uses completeness (∑c_l = 1) and simplicity (smulRange is ⊥ or ⊤) for existence by contradiction.

2. **hblock_unique** (PROVED): The active block is unique. If two blocks l₁, l₂ both act as identity, orthogonality (c_{l₁}·c_{l₂} = 0) gives a contradiction via surjectivity of one block and zero product.

3. **rank-0 case** (PROVED): E₁₁ in block σ(i) acts as 0 on M_j for i ≠ j. Key: Pi.single (σ i) E₁₁ = c_{σ(i)} · Pi.single (σ i) E₁₁, and c_{σ(i)} annihilates M_j (since σ(i) ≠ σ(j)), so E₁₁ annihilates M_j too.

Also added helper lemmas: `completeOrthogonalIdempotents_pi_single_one` and `pi_single_one_comm` for central idempotent properties in product rings.
