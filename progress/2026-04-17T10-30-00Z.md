## Current frontier

Three `sorry`s remain in the proof:

1. **N-invariance of W₁(4)** under `nilpotentShiftLin` (HARD - the core blocker)
2. **N-invariance of W₂(4)** (symmetric to above)
3. **Propagation**: W(4) = ⊥ → all W(v) = ⊥

### Why N-invariance is hard

The nilpotent enters through arm 1's map `etilde7Arm1Embed: (p,q) ↦ (p+q, p, 0, Nq)`, which couples blocks A, B, and D of the 4-block center. However, leaves 4 and 7 connect ONLY to block A (via `(x,0,0,0)`). The other blocks B, C, D are reached only through intermediate or arm-1 vertices.

In the star proof (5 vertices, 2-block center), N-invariance was trivial because:
- Leaf 4 maps x ↦ (x, Nx) into the SAME 2 blocks as leaf 3 maps x ↦ (x, x)
- The core decomposition directly gives: x ∈ W(4) → Nx ∈ W(2) = W(1)

In Ẽ₇, the nilpotent's output (block D) and the leaf's block (A) are independent positions in the 4-block center. No amount of complement decomposition at the center directly connects them without also constraining the intermediate vertices (3, 6) and arm 1 vertex (1).

### Approaches attempted and why they failed

1. **Block B,D projection**: tried to project the center to (B,D) blocks to get a 2-block sub-problem. Failed because no vertex maps purely to blocks (C,D) — all touch block A.

2. **Vertex 1 complement decomposition**: decomposed (0,x) and (x,0) at vertex 1, extracted arm1 images at center. Generated many elements but couldn't constrain them without knowing the structure of W₁(1).

3. **Intermediate vertex containment (W₁(3) ≤ W₁(6))**: arm2 and arm3 chains from the same intermediate element go to DIFFERENT center positions — (a,b,0,0) vs (a,0,b,0) — so the leaf_containment trick doesn't generalize beyond the first block.

4. **Third block of W₁(5)**: tried to show (0,0,x) ∈ W₁(5) for x ∈ S, which would give (0,0,0,x) at center. Circular — requires the very N-invariance being proved.

### Propagation difficulty

Even ASSUMING N-invariance, the propagation W(4) = ⊥ → all W(v) = ⊥ is non-trivial for Ẽ₇ because:
- The center has 4 blocks but leaves only cover block A
- To show W'(center) = ⊤, need blocks B,C,D also covered
- Block D is reached ONLY through arm 1 (Nq term) — requires arm 1 contribution to be full
- This is intertwined with the N-invariance problem

## Next step

The N-invariance problem for Ẽ₇ likely requires one of:
1. **A different representation**: The current maps may not be the "right" ones from the book. The arm 1 map couples (A,B,D) while arms 2/3 only touch A from leaves. A representation where arm 1 couples blocks that are ALSO reachable from leaves might make N-invariance provable.
2. **A different proof technique**: Instead of the star/D̃₅ pattern, try a technique that works with 4-block centers (e.g., direct rank/dimension arguments on the submodule lattice).
3. **Subgraph embedding**: D̃₅ = T_{1,2,2} embeds into Ẽ₇ = T_{1,3,3}. Use `subgraph_infinite_type_transfer` to prove `etilde7_not_finite_type` without needing `etilde7Rep_isIndecomposable`.

Option 3 is the most pragmatic — it bypasses the hard proof entirely by using the existing D̃₅ infrastructure.
