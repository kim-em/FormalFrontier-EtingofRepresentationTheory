### Proof structure

Given `Пғ` column-standard, `qвӮҖ вҲҲ Q_О»`, and
`tabloidCumulCount la Пғ k i < tabloidCumulCount la (w * qвӮҖвҒ»В№ * Пғ) k i`,
the proof produces two distinct positions in the same column with
coincident `w`-row. Strategy:

1. **By contradiction**: assume `(col, row вҲҳ w)` is injective on `Fin n`.
2. **Column decomposition** via `Finset.card_eq_sum_card_fiberwise` on the
   fiber `col(Пғ e)`. Reduces to per-column inequality `|B_c| вүӨ |A_c|`.
3. **Bound on `|B_c|`**: the map `e вҶҰ row (w * qвӮҖвҒ»В№ * Пғ) e` is injective
   on `B_c` (using the preservation of columns by `qвӮҖвҒ»В№`) with image in
   `Finset.range i`, so `|B_c| вүӨ i`. Combined with `|B_c| вүӨ |E_c|`,
   this gives `|B_c| вүӨ min(E_c.card, i)`.
4. **Characterization of `|A_c|`**: for Пғ column-standard, the map
   `e вҶҰ row(Пғ e)` is (a) injective on `E_c` (via
   `rowOfPos_colOfPos_injective` on column-`c`), (b) its image is
   downward-closed (via col-std + `exists_pos_of_cell` +
   partition sortedness). Hence the image equals `Finset.range E_c.card`.
   Then `A_c = E_c.filter (row < i)` has image `Finset.range (min E_c.card i)`,
   so `|A_c| = min(E_c.card, i)`.
5. Sum per-column inequalities; `omega` closes the contradiction.

### Key techniques

- **Column preservation** of `qвӮҖ вҲҲ ColumnSubgroup`: used
  `(ColumnSubgroup n la).inv_mem hqвӮҖ_col` to get `qвӮҖвҒ»В№ вҲҲ Q_О»`, which
  preserves columns.
- **Downward closure via col-std**: for `e вҲҲ E_c` and `r' вүӨ rowFn e`,
  used `List.pairwise_iff_getElem` on `la.parts.pairwise_sort (В· вүҘ В·)` to
  show `parts.getD r' 0 вүҘ parts.getD (rowFn e) 0`, then `colOfPos_lt_getD`
  + `exists_pos_of_cell` to construct the earlier-row position.
- **Bijection on column**: `rowOfPos_colOfPos_injective` is the core
  tool for injectivity on a fixed column.
