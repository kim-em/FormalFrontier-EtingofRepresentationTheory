Two major Schur-Weyl equivariance PRs merged in quick succession in the
last wave:

- **PR #2538** (`feat(Ch5 #2527 Part A): bridge equivariance —
  polyToTensor_rightTransl + homogeneousPolyToTensor_equivariant`,

  (`homogeneousPolyToTensor` in `Chapter5/PolynomialTensorBridge.lean`)
  with GL_N-equivariance.

- **PR #2539** (`feat(Ch5 #2537): polynomialRep_embeds_in_tensorPower
  equivariance (#2527 Part B)`, +357 lines): assembles the
  GL_N-equivariant embedding at
  `Chapter5/PolynomialRepEmbedding.lean:474`, bundling the equivariance
  assembly via `splitDualBasis_tgtGLAction`,
  `matrixCoeffPoly_polyRightTransl`, `polyTensorRow_equivariant`, and
  `polyTensorBundle_equivariant`.

Together these two PRs deliver the equivariance layer that **all
downstream Schur-Weyl work depends on** — specifically #2482 (Schur-Weyl
#5), which consumes `polynomialRep_embeds_in_tensorPower` as its core
embedding step; #2483 (Schur-Weyl #6) then consumes #2482.

Given the critical-path position and the non-trivial bundle of
equivariance lemmas (each with its own action-preservation and
tensor-basis unfolding), an independent audit before the downstream
theorems are stated and proved is worth the cycles. Any drift here
would cascade into #2482/#2483 and force rework at the consumption
site.

Of the two, **PR #2539 is higher risk**: it takes hypotheses from a
user-supplied existential (`hP`, `hP_mul`) whose interaction with
`ρ.map_mul` is subtle; the `Path B` design choice (hypothesizing the
polynomial identity rather than deriving it — see the theorem's
docstring at `Chapter5/PolynomialRepEmbedding.lean:456-473`) means the
equivariance conclusion is **conditional** on a polynomial identity
that is provable but not yet proved in-project. That conditional bears
review.
