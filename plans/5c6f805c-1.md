   964) using the classical Garnir-term dominance argument:

   a. Unfold `twistedPolytabloid w σ` as Σ_{q ∈ Q_λ} sign(q)·[w·q⁻¹·σ].
   b. For each q, column-normalize the tabloid w·q⁻¹·σ: apply
      `exists_column_standard_mul` to get q_{w,q} ∈ Q_λ with
      q_{w,q}·(w·q⁻¹·σ) column-standard, then use
      `generalizedPolytabloidTab_col_mul` to rewrite
      `ψ_{w·q⁻¹·σ}` as sign(q_{w,q})·ψ_{q_{w,q}·w·q⁻¹·σ}.
      (Note: the sum is over tabloids — the `[· · ·]` notation lives
      at the tabloid level, so step (b) may need to go through the
      `Finsupp.single` ≡ `generalizedPolytabloidTab` intermediate.)
   c. Prove the **classical Garnir-term dominance**: for each q, the
      col-standardized tabloid τ_{w,q} = q_{w,q}·w·q⁻¹·σ satisfies
      either (i) `tabloidStrictDominates la σ τ_{w,q}` (σ strictly
      dominates τ_{w,q}), or (ii) τ_{w,q}.tabloid = σ.tabloid and
      `rowInvCount' τ_{w,q} < rowInvCount' σ`.
      The (ii) case arises only when w·q⁻¹ ∈ P_λ (row-preserving),
      which is excluded by `hw_row : w ∉ RowSubgroup` — except through
      conjugation by q. Reference: James, "The Representation Theory
      of the Symmetric Groups", Ch. 7; Fulton, "Young Tableaux", Ch. 7.
   d. Collect each term into `Submodule.span` via `Submodule.sum_mem`
      and `Submodule.smul_mem`, witnessing membership through the
      subtype constructor in the span's range.
