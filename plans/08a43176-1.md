### Q1 ‚Äî `schurEvaluationEquiv_apply_tmul` correctness
(`Theorem5_18_1.lean:179`-213)

The lemma claims `schurEvaluationEquiv k A V M h (v ‚äó‚Çú f) = f v` for any

`A`-linear `f : V ‚Üí‚Çó[A] M`.

- Follow the proof. The key step rewrites the underlying map as
  `e.symm (fun j => (endOfSimpleEquivAlgClosed k A V).symm
                      ((LinearMap.proj j).comp (e.toLinearMap.comp f)) ‚Ä¢ v)`.
  This `step1 := rfl` claim hinges on the exact construction of the
  five-step chain in `schurEvaluationEquiv` (lines 153‚Äì170). Verify
  the `rfl` is honest (i.e. each step really is `defeq` after `let`
  binding) and not hiding a transport.
- Is the helper `endOfSimpleEquivAlgClosed_symm_smul_apply` invoked
  with the correct semantics? In particular,
  `(endOfSimpleEquivAlgClosed).symm œÜ ‚Ä¢ v = œÜ v` requires
  `endOfSimpleEquivAlgClosed k A V` to be `End_A V ‚Üí k` rather than
  `End_A V ‚Üí End_A V`. Confirm.
- The closing `e.symm_apply_apply (f v)` step relies on the previous
  `funext` rewrite to `e (f v)`. Verify that `hv j` correctly identifies
  `(LinearMap.proj j).comp (e.toLinearMap.comp f) v = e (f v) j` (this
  is the projection of `e (f v)` onto its `j`-th component).
- **Acceptance criterion**: the lemma actually proves the displayed
  equation, with no scalar-factor / sign / `congr`-noise drift.
