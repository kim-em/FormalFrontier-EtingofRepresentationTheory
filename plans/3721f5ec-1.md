Session `add8c41f` (skip-comment on #2533, 2026-04-27) showed Step 1 is
**unsound as stated**:

- The natural `S_n` action on `TabloidRepresentation` (induced by
  `tabloidProjection`) is right translation by `wÔü╗┬╣`, not left
  translation. So `w ÔÇó ¤ł_¤â = ÔłĹ_q sign(q) ┬Ě ╬┤_{[qÔü╗┬╣ ¤â wÔü╗┬╣]}`, not
  `ÔłĹ_q sign(q) ┬Ě ╬┤_{[w qÔü╗┬╣ ¤â]} = TP(w, ¤â)`.
- "Left translation by `w`" on tabloid classes is not a well-defined
  linear map (it requires `w ┬Ě P_╬╗ ┬Ě wÔü╗┬╣ = P_╬╗`).
- A direct route via
  `TP = (1/|P_╬╗|) ┬Ě tabloidProjection(of(¤âÔü╗┬╣) ┬Ě ColAntisym ┬Ě of(wÔü╗┬╣) ┬Ě RowSym)`
  is also blocked: `of(¤âÔü╗┬╣) ┬Ě ColAntisym ┬Ě of(wÔü╗┬╣) ┬Ě RowSym` is not in
  `V_╬╗ = Ôäé[S_n] ┬Ě YoungSym` (RowSym sits to the right of ColAntisym;
  wrong factorization).
- A bypass that does Algorithm A directly on TP using col-std reps
  fails because col-std reps don't always exist for tabloids in
  `supp(TP)` (counter-example for ╬╗=(2,2): tabloid `[{2,3}|{0,1}]`
  has no col-std representative ÔÇö its row-sorted form `[2,3,0,1]`
  has columns `(2,0)` and `(3,1)` both decreasing).
- An SYT-based variant of Algorithm A requires that every
  dominance-maximal element of `supp(v)` along the recursion be an
  SYT-tabloid, which holds *because* `v Ôłł span polytabloidTab T` ÔÇö
  circular with the conclusion we're trying to prove.

So **`TP Ôłł Submodule.span Ôäé (Set.range polytabloidTab)` is not a small
upstream lemma**: it is essentially equivalent to half of the Specht
basis theorem (`tabloidProjection_spechtModule_le_span`,
`SpechtModuleBasis.lean:2310`, currently downstream of us). The math
is correct (`TP` IS classically in `span polytabloidTab T` ÔÇö verified

but the proof requires either a restructuring of the recursion or a
genuinely different approach.
