**Problem 2.16.3.** (Hard!) For any element $x$ of a Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ let $\operatorname{ad}(x)$ denote the operator $\mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g}$, $y \mapsto [x, y]$. Consider the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}_n$ generated by two elements $x, y$ with the defining relations $\operatorname{ad}(x)^2(y) = \operatorname{ad}(y)^{n+1}(x) = 0$.

(a) Show that the Lie algebras $\mathfrak{g}_1, \mathfrak{g}_2, \mathfrak{g}_3$ are finite dimensional and find their dimensions.

(b) (Harder!) Show that the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}_4$ has infinite dimension. Construct explicitly a basis of this algebra.
