
**Theorem 2.1.1.** *Let $k = \mathbb{C}$ be the field of complex numbers. Then:*

*(i) The algebra $U$ has exactly one irreducible representation $V_d$ of each dimension, up to equivalence; this representation is realized in the space of homogeneous polynomials of two variables $x, y$ of degree $d - 1$ and is defined by the formulas*

$$\rho(h) = x\frac{\partial}{\partial x} - y\frac{\partial}{\partial y}, \quad \rho(e) = x\frac{\partial}{\partial y}, \quad \rho(f) = y\frac{\partial}{\partial x}.$$

*(ii) Any indecomposable finite dimensional representation of $U$ is irreducible. That is, any finite dimensional representation of $U$ is a direct sum of irreducible representations.*
