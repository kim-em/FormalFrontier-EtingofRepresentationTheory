## 9.7. Morita equivalence

In this section we will discuss the theory of Morita equivalence of finite dimensional algebras. We note that this theory extends with appropriate changes to the infinite dimensional case.

Theorem 9.6.4 shows that a finite abelian category $\mathcal{C}$ can be identified with the category of finite dimensional modules over a finite dimensional algebra $B$, once we choose a projective generator $P$ of $\mathcal{C}$. However, the projective generator is not unique. Namely, let $P_1, \ldots, P_m$ be the indecomposable projective objects of $\mathcal{C}$ (they make sense by Theorem 9.6.4). Then all the projective generators of $\mathcal{C}$ are the objects of the form $P_{\mathbf{n}} := \bigoplus_{i=1}^m n_i P_i$, where $n_i \geq 1$ (check it!). Defining $B_{\mathbf{n}} = B_{\mathbf{n}}(\mathcal{C}) := \operatorname{End}(P_{\mathbf{n}})^{\mathrm{op}}$, we see that the algebras $B_{\mathbf{n}}$ are all the finite dimensional algebras whose category of finite dimensional modules is equivalent to $\mathcal{C}$.
